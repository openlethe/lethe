# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Lethe contributors should make the project technically rigorous and professionally welcoming. Treat other participants with respect, discuss evidence rather than personalities, and assume good faith while still challenging unsafe designs.

Expected behavior includes:

- giving actionable, specific review feedback;
- acknowledging uncertainty and correcting mistakes;
- respecting privacy and security-reporting boundaries;
- welcoming contributors with different backgrounds and experience levels;
- keeping technical disagreements focused on the work.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate disclosure of private information, credential sharing, retaliation against security reporters, or sustained disruption of project spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, commits, issues, or other contributions that violate this standard. Responses may range from a private warning to temporary or permanent exclusion, depending on severity and pattern.

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainers through an appropriate non-public GitHub channel. Do not place sensitive personal information in a public issue.

This policy applies in project repositories, review discussions, release spaces, and other venues where someone is representing Lethe.
